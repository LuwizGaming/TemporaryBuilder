    <div class="bio-section">
      <h2>Early Life</h2>
      <p>
        Born in Badoc, Ilocos Norte, Juan Luna was the third of seven children.
        He studied in Manila and showed early talent in painting and drawing.
        He later traveled to Spain to pursue formal art education and developed
        his academic style under European influence.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="bio-section">
      <h2>Travel Abroad</h2>
      <p>
        Luna studied in Madrid and Rome, where he was exposed to Renaissance art.
        He gained recognition in European exhibitions, including winning a silver
        medal for La Muerte de Cleopatra in 1881.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="bio-section">
      <h2>Artistic Career</h2>
      <p>
        His masterpiece Spoliarium won a gold medal at the 1884 Madrid Exposition,
        bringing international fame. His works often depicted historical and
        political themes with emotional intensity and classical composition.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="bio-section">
      <h2>Marriage</h2>
      <p>
        He married María de la Paz Pardo de Tavera in 1886. Their marriage
        became troubled and ended in tragedy in 1892, leading to his arrest
        and later acquittal under a crime-of-passion ruling.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="bio-section">
      <h2>Final Years</h2>
      <p>
        Luna returned to the Philippines, was involved during the revolution era,
        and later served in diplomatic efforts for the Philippine Republic.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="bio-section">
      <h2>Death</h2>
      <p>
        He died in Hong Kong in 1899 due to cardiac arrest. He is remembered as
        one of the greatest Filipino painters in history.
      </p>
    </div>
